Rules

Rounds and Turns

There are 4 rounds, each representing a different metro line and starting from a different station. Each round consists of 8 turns where you will draw cards to build your subway network.

Drawing Cards

You will draw a card at each turn. Your card will either have the letter A, B, C, D or a Joker symbol. Each letter corresponds to a specific type of station on the metro line you are allowed to connect to, while the Joker can be used to connect to any station.

  • Drawing a segment is always optional. If you cannot or don’t want to draw, you may simply draw the next card.
  • Each segment must be a straight line, either horizontal, vertical or diagonal at 45°.
  • In a round, the first segment must start from the starting station of the current line and end at a station whose type matches the drawn card.
  • Every subsequent segment must start from one of the current line’s endpoints and end at a station whose type matches the card.
  • Each segment must connect exactly two stations.
  • A segment cannot pass through a third station.
  • Segments cannot cross other segments in open areas — only at stations.
  • A line cannot re-enter a station it has already visited. However, you may connect to stations already used by other lines — these become busy junctions.
  • You cannot draw multiple segments between the same two stations (no parallel segments), even between different lines.
  • The station "Deák tér" acts as a Joker — any card may connect to it, as long as other rules are followed.

Scoring

Scoring Each Round

At the end of each round, your score for that round is calculated based on the following

  • ND = Number of districts this line passes through
  • NS = Number of stations it touches in the district where it has the most stations
  • CD = Number of times it crosses the Danube
  • RS = (ND × NS) + CD → this round’s score

Final Score Calculation

  • Rounds: sum of all RS values → Sum(RS) = (RS1 + RS2 + RS3 + RS4)
  • Railway stations: every time a line enters a railway station, the Railway Stations Scoring Scale goes up. The maximum it can get to is 25 points after entering a railway station 10 times. This value (NR) is added to the final score once.
  • Junctions: We count how many stations are served by two different metro lines (J2), how many by three different lines (J3), and how many by all four metro lines (J4).
  • Final score = Sum(FP) + PP + (2 × CSP2) + (5 × CSP3) + (9 × CSP4)

Fortunately, we calculate your score for you so you can focus on building the best subway network possible!
